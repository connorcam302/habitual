# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Connor — solo user, daily. Two primary contexts: quick morning check (what's on today?) and evening session logging (mark done, log how it felt). Uses it on iPhone as a full-screen PWA and on desktop in a browser. Fluency matters more than discoverability; no onboarding needed.

## Product Purpose

Track a fixed weekly fitness and Mandarin learning schedule. Primary job on any given visit: see what's scheduled and mark sessions done. Secondary job: log felt quality and notes after physical sessions. Success looks like the app quietly supporting a consistent training routine without demanding attention.

## Brand Personality

Athletic, premium, clean. Performance tool built for one person's serious routine — like a well-made training tool you'd pay for, not a consumer wellness app. Sharp and precise, never loud.

## Anti-references

- Generic fitness apps (MyFitnessPal, Strava): blue gradients, gamified rings, streak counters, motivational copy, social layer
- Productivity SaaS (Notion, ClickUp, Todoist): cream / light-mode, feature-heavy interfaces, generic card grids, corporate-tool aesthetic

## Design Principles

1. **Ritual over gamification** — no badges, streaks, or motivational nudges; the schedule is the commitment, the app just records it
2. **Data at a glance** — the primary interaction is seeing the week and tapping; every extra tap is friction against the habit
3. **Built for one** — design for fluency, not discoverability; skip affordances that only help new users
4. **Earn the premium** — dark, type-forward, precise; quality through restraint, not decoration
5. **Responsive to the body** — large tap targets, safe-area aware, legible at 6am half-awake

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Single user, personal use. No formal WCAG target. Aim for informal WCAG AA contrast ratios and generously sized tap targets (min 44×44px). No specific reduced-motion requirements noted, but avoid gratuitous animation.
