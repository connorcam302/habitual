# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Connor — solo user, daily. Two primary contexts: quick morning check (what's on today?) and evening session logging (mark done, log how it felt). Uses it on iPhone as a full-screen PWA and on desktop in a browser. Fluency matters more than discoverability; no onboarding needed.

## Product Purpose

Track a fixed weekly fitness and Mandarin learning schedule. Primary job on any given visit: see what's scheduled and mark sessions done. Secondary job: log felt quality and notes after physical sessions. AI week planning and in-session workout briefings are support features, not the centre of gravity. Success looks like the app quietly supporting a consistent training routine without demanding attention.

## Brand Personality

Athletic, personal, warm. Built for one person's real training life — the kind of tool that feels like it belongs to you after a week of use, not a product you're borrowing. Precise and direct, never corporate. The warmth of a well-worn kit bag, not the sheen of a SaaS dashboard. Never cold, never performatively minimal, never trying to impress anyone who isn't using it.

## Anti-references

- Generic fitness apps (MyFitnessPal, Strava): blue gradients, gamified rings, streak counters, motivational copy, social layer
- Productivity SaaS (Notion, ClickUp, Todoist): cream / light-mode, feature-heavy interfaces, generic card grids, corporate-tool aesthetic
- AI products (Cursor, Perplexity, any LLM interface): cobalt-to-violet gradients, cold dark surfaces with blue-purple undertones, glowing accents, "AI-coded" color palettes. The app uses AI as a tool; it must not look like an AI product.

## Design Principles

1. **Ritual over gamification** — no badges, streaks, or motivational nudges; the schedule is the commitment, the app just records it
2. **Data at a glance** — the primary interaction is seeing the week and tapping; every extra tap is friction against the habit
3. **Built for one** — design for fluency, not discoverability; skip affordances that only help new users
4. **Feels owned, not rented** — warmth and personality over polish and abstraction; it should look like a tool Connor made for himself, not a product designed for a market
5. **Responsive to the body** — large tap targets, safe-area aware, legible at 6am half-awake

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Single user, personal use. No formal WCAG target. Aim for informal WCAG AA contrast ratios and generously sized tap targets (min 44×44px). No specific reduced-motion requirements noted, but avoid gratuitous animation.
